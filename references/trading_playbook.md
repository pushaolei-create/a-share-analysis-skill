# Trading Playbook

This note distills recurring short-term and swing-trading ideas from the user-provided Chinese stock-trading PDFs into reusable decision rules. Treat these as setup filters and risk controls, not mechanical guarantees.

## Core Principle

Prefer setups where structure, volume, and market regime agree with each other. Avoid acting on a single candlestick, a single indicator, or a title-level pattern name without confirming context.

## Regime First

Classify the market and the stock before discussing entries:

- Trend advance: higher highs, higher lows, rising moving averages, pullbacks that shrink in volume.
- Range or shakeout: price oscillates near a support box, headlines feel noisy, and false breaks are common.
- Exhaustion or distribution: large upside bars arrive after an extended run, turnover expands too late, and follow-through weakens.
- Rebound from oversold: sharp drop slows, panic volume fades, and price starts reclaiming key averages or prior breakdown levels.

Match tactics to regime:

- In strong trends, buy confirmation on orderly pullbacks or base breakouts.
- In ranges, be more selective and reduce target size.
- In late-stage blow-off moves, think first about protection and exit quality.

## Big Yang Line Context

Large bullish candles are useful only when they appear after preparation. Favor them when most of the following are true:

- The stock spent time building a base or tightening range.
- Downside probes failed and were repaired quickly.
- Turnover expands on the breakout bar but was not already overheated for many sessions.
- The move clears a visible resistance level, not just intraday noise.
- The sector or theme is also active.

Be cautious when a large bullish candle appears:

- Straight after a multi-day vertical chase.
- Far above moving-average support.
- Without volume confirmation.
- Against weak market breadth or a collapsing peer group.

## Shakeout And Washout Recognition

Use washout language only when price action shows both pressure and recovery:

- Intraday break of support followed by close back above the key level.
- Long lower shadow after prior holders were forced out.
- Pullback volume contracts compared with the prior advance.
- Reclaim of VWAP, prior close, or an important moving average after a weak open.

Signs that a move is not a healthy washout:

- Price loses support and stays below it into the close.
- Volume expands on the down move and remains heavy on weak rebounds.
- Multiple failed reclaim attempts appear in a short window.
- The broader market is in risk-off rotation and the stock lacks a catalyst.

## Moving-Average Use

The 60-day moving average is best used as a trend filter and behavior reference, not as a magic line.

Favor bullish interpretations when:

- The 60-day average is rising or has flattened after a long decline.
- Price reclaims the line and holds it on retest.
- Shorter averages align upward after the reclaim.
- Volume expands on the reclaim and contracts on the pullback.

Treat the setup as weaker when:

- Price repeatedly pierces the 60-day line without directional follow-through.
- The line is still falling sharply and every rebound stalls beneath it.
- Breakout attempts occur with low participation.

## Buy Point Framework

Split buy points into three types:

- Breakout buy: above base resistance, platform high, or major average with volume confirmation.
- Pullback buy: after a breakout, price retests support without abnormal selling pressure.
- Reversal buy: after capitulation or an extended decline, price reclaims a decisive level and confirms with follow-through.

Require each buy discussion to answer:

- What level confirms the setup?
- What invalidates it?
- What evidence shows demand is improving?
- Is the broader tape helping or hurting?

## Sell And Trim Framework

Do not discuss entry without a matching exit idea.

Use these exit triggers:

- Structural failure: loss of support, failed breakout, or close back into the base.
- Momentum fade: large upside bar with no follow-through, repeated upper shadows, or volume climax near resistance.
- Objective reached: price runs into a planned target, stretched extension, or major event risk.
- Thesis break: catalyst weakens, sector leadership rotates away, or new disclosure changes the setup.

For strong stocks, prefer scaling and trailing over all-in or all-out language unless the user explicitly asks for hard-stop planning.

## Intraday T Trading

For "do T" discussions, keep the bar high:

- Only consider T trading in liquid names with clear intraday rhythm.
- Use prior-day high or low, VWAP, opening range, and obvious intraday supply zones.
- Sell part into strength only when the stock is extended relative to its own intraday structure.
- Buy back only when the retrace shows exhaustion and reclaim signals, not simply because price is lower.
- Avoid forcing T trades in narrow ranges, weak liquidity, or event-driven gaps.

## Small Loss, Large Gain Discipline

Convert slogans into rules:

- Risk the least when the setup is least proven.
- Add only after confirmation, not to rescue a broken entry.
- Keep invalidation level close enough that a wrong trade stays small.
- Let target logic come from structure, volatility, and event path rather than hope.

## Short-Term Versus Mid-Term

Separate the time frame before giving advice:

- Short-term focus: momentum, liquidity, catalyst window, breakout quality, and intraday behavior.
- Mid-term focus: trend persistence, earnings path, valuation support, and repeated support tests.

Do not mix a short-term entry with a mid-term stop or vice versa.

## How To Use In Responses

When the user asks for a technical read on a stock, answer in this order:

1. Regime and context.
2. Setup description.
3. Confirmation trigger.
4. Failure point.
5. Risk or position-sizing caveat.

Avoid certainty words such as "must rise", "will launch", or "guaranteed washout success".
