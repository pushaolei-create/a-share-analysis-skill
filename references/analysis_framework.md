# A-Share Analysis Framework

## Market Overview

Use this checklist for broad A-share analysis:

1. Market state: compare the SSE Composite, SZSE Component, ChiNext Index, STAR 50, BSE 50, CSI 300, CSI 500, and CSI 1000 when relevant.
2. Breadth: rising versus falling stocks, limit-up and limit-down count, new highs and lows, turnover distribution, and style split across large-cap, mid-cap, small-cap, dividend, growth, cyclical, and thematic names.
3. Liquidity: total turnover, margin financing balance, money-market rates, PBOC operations, ETF flows, mutual fund issuance, and northbound or southbound flow where available.
4. Policy and macro: State Council, CSRC, exchanges, PBOC, NDRC, MIIT, fiscal policy, PMI, CPI or PPI, credit data, exports, property, and industrial profits.
5. Sector rotation: rank industries by relative strength, turnover share, earnings revision, policy catalyst, and crowdedness.
6. Scenario map: bullish, base, and bearish paths with observable triggers.

## Sector Or Theme Analysis

Cover:

- Demand driver: policy, capex cycle, consumer recovery, export demand, technology adoption, or price cycle.
- Supply driver: capacity, inventory, raw materials, utilization, regulation, or substitution risk.
- Profit transmission: who captures margin, who bears cost pressure, and where operating leverage sits.
- Representative stocks: leaders, challengers, upstream or downstream beneficiaries, and high-beta names.
- Crowdedness and timing: volume surge, gap openings, limit-up chains, analyst revision intensity, and event calendar.

## Single-Stock Analysis

Use this order:

1. Identify the company: ticker, exchange, stock name, industry, actual controller, main products, and revenue geography.
2. Event check: latest annual, interim, or quarterly report, performance forecast, major contract, M&A, pledge, litigation, regulatory inquiry, share unlock, buyback, dividend, or management change.
3. Fundamentals: revenue growth, gross margin, operating margin, net margin, ROE, asset turnover, leverage, working capital, receivables, inventory, operating cash flow, capex, and free cash flow.
4. Quality of earnings: compare net profit with operating cash flow, non-recurring gains, impairment, fair-value changes, capitalization, and related-party transactions.
5. Valuation: PE, PB, PS, and EV/EBITDA where meaningful; compare with history, peers, growth, ROE, dividend yield, and cycle position.
6. Catalysts: earnings inflection, policy support, product cycle, capacity release, price hike, order visibility, asset restructuring, index inclusion, or capital return.
7. Risks: demand miss, policy tightening, price decline, margin compression, governance risk, pledge or liquidity risk, customer concentration, technology substitution, FX, commodities, or delisting or ST risk.
8. Technical context: trend, moving averages, support and resistance, volume-price divergence, gap behavior, volatility, and stop reference if the user asks for a trading frame.

## Rating Language

Avoid unsupported buy or sell commands. Prefer:

- Constructive, neutral, or cautious.
- Watch for confirmation above or below named levels.
- Attractive only if valuation and catalysts compensate for stated risks.
- Thesis invalidated if named operating or market signals fail.
