# Data And Compliance Notes

## Preferred Sources

Official and primary sources:

- Company filings and announcements: CNINFO, SSE, SZSE, and BSE.
- Regulation and enforcement: CSRC, the exchanges, and China Securities Depository and Clearing.
- Macro and policy: National Bureau of Statistics, PBOC, Ministry of Finance, NDRC, and MIIT.
- Index methodology and constituents: CSI Index, SSE Index, SZSE Information, and BSE Index.

Market and secondary sources:

- Quotes, valuation snapshots, money flow, and chart data: use provider names and timestamps.
- Consensus estimates: identify the provider and date; note that coverage can be sparse for small-cap or BSE names.
- News: prefer original announcements; use media reports only as secondary context.

## Citation Rules

- Cite the filing title, announcement date, reporting period, and exchange or disclosure platform.
- For prices and flows, cite the market data provider and quote time.
- If data conflicts, explain the discrepancy instead of averaging silently.
- Use exact units: RMB, shares, lots, and percentage points. Use Chinese large-number units only when the context is clear.

## Financial Safety

- Make clear that the output is research and analysis, not individualized financial advice.
- Do not guarantee returns, price targets, or trading success.
- Separate facts, estimates, and opinions.
- Call out liquidity, suspension, ST or delisting, and daily price-limit risks for A-shares.
- For user-provided positions, discuss risk exposure and scenario planning rather than issuing an unconditional instruction.
